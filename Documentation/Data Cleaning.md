## Data Cleaning:-

Data cleaning is a crucial step before analyzing any dataset.  
Raw data often contains issues such as:
- Formatting inconsistencies  
- Unnecessary spaces  
- Incorrect or inconsistent values  

If we directly analyze such data, it can lead to inaccurate or misleading results.



## Key Concepts Learned:-

### 1. Formatting
Sometimes text data may have inconsistent capitalization (e.g., `"india"`, `"India"`, `"INDIA"`).  
To ensure uniformity, formatting is applied to standardize the data.


### 2. Handling Incorrect or Inconsistent Values
Data may contain:
- Wrong entries  
- Duplicate representations of the same value  

These are corrected or replaced to maintain consistency across the dataset.


### 3. TRIM and CLEAN Functions

- **TRIM**: Removes unnecessary spaces from text (except single spaces between words).  
- **CLEAN**: Removes non-printable or hidden characters that may cause errors during analysis.  



## Benefits of Data Cleaning:-

Data cleaning improves:
- Accuracy  
- Consistency  
- Reliability of the dataset  

It ensures that further analysis is meaningful and trustworthy.
